# OCEAN Trait Rubric — AlterEgo Build Pipeline

Used in pairwise comparison prompts. Defines what each trait means in behavioural terms,
and gives anchor examples so Claude applies the scale consistently across franchises.

---

## Openness to Experience

**High:** Curious, imaginative, drawn to novelty and ideas. Seeks out new perspectives,
enjoys complexity, comfortable with ambiguity. Often unconventional in thinking or values.

**Low:** Practical, conventional, prefers routine and the familiar. Values proven methods
over innovation. Down-to-earth; not drawn to abstract theorising.

*When comparing:* Look for evidence of intellectual curiosity, creativity, willingness to
question norms, or comfort with change vs. preference for the known and traditional.

---

## Conscientiousness

**High:** Organised, disciplined, reliable, plans ahead. Follows through on commitments.
Values duty, order, and precision. Self-controlled even under pressure.

**Low:** Spontaneous, flexible, or careless. Dislikes rigid structure; may procrastinate
or improvise rather than plan. Can be creative but unreliable.

*When comparing:* Look for evidence of preparation, rule-following, keeping promises,
attention to detail, or their opposites — impulsiveness and disorder.

---

## Extraversion

**High:** Energised by others; seeks social interaction, dominates conversations,
assertive, expressive, enjoys being the centre of attention. Optimistic, enthusiastic.

**Low (Introversion):** Energised by solitude; reserved, quiet, prefers small groups or
one-on-one. Not necessarily shy — simply less driven by social reward.

*When comparing:* Look for seeking vs. avoiding social situations, talkativeness,
dominance in group settings, expressiveness, or preference for solitude.

---

## Agreeableness

**High:** Cooperative, warm, trusting, empathetic, avoids conflict. Puts others' needs
first. Generous, willing to compromise. Believes in people's basic goodness.

**Low:** Competitive, sceptical, blunt, self-interested. Will pursue goals at others'
expense. Not necessarily cruel — just not driven by harmony or others' approval.

*When comparing:* Look for cooperation vs. competition, empathy vs. indifference,
trust vs. suspicion, warmth vs. coldness in dealings with others.

---

## Neuroticism  *(note: IPIP labels this "Emotional Stability" — the inverse)*

**High Neuroticism:** Prone to anxiety, mood swings, worry, anger, or emotional
volatility. Reacts strongly to stress. Difficulty recovering from setbacks.

**Low Neuroticism (High Emotional Stability):** Calm, even-tempered, resilient under
pressure. Doesn't dwell on difficulties; recovers quickly from adversity.

*When comparing:* Look for emotional reactions to setbacks, anxiety before conflict,
anger or grief responses, composure under pressure, or resilience vs. fragility.

---

## Notes for prompt use

- Compare **behaviours and decisions shown in the chapter** — not reputation or backstory
  unless it's demonstrated in the text.
- A character can be temporarily distressed without being high-Neuroticism; look for
  *patterns* across the chapter.
- "Tie" is a valid answer when both characters show equal evidence or neither is
  meaningfully present for a given trait.
- Always justify with a one-line reason citing specific chapter evidence.
